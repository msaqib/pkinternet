# Findings 1.3 - Nayatel Routing (what it transits)

**Author:** Rayan Atif
**Source:** Experiment 01 traceroutes from the Nayatel probe (AS23674), 91 destinations.

Companion to `findings/01_hosting_and_routing_analysis.md`. Transit is detected by
BGP ASN + RDAP registry name (Transworld's backbone is unannounced, so the RDAP
label is used). "Transit path" lists the operators between Nayatel and the
destination (private/CGNAT and Nayatel-own hops removed).

## Summary

| Path type | destinations | share |
|---|---|---|
| **Transworld (LDI)** | 35 | 38% |
| **PTCL (LDI)** | 2 | 2% |
| **No LDI** | 54 | 59% |

So **~40%** of Nayatel paths use an LDI (almost all Transworld), and **~59% avoid
the LDIs entirely**.

## What the "no LDI" paths use

1. **Direct peering with Cloudflare (~38)** - Nayatel straight to Cloudflare (~3 ms).
2. **Direct peering with Microsoft (3)** - aku.edu, bankalfalah, hum.tv.
3. **Direct domestic peering with PK networks (~7)** - Multinet (dunyanews directly),
   PERN (hec, pu), PITB (punjab), and COMSATS/Multinet via `HTISPL-PK` (pid, pseb).
4. **Its OWN international transit, NOT Transworld (3)** - daraz via SingTel (Singapore)
   to Alibaba; mcb and iba via NTT (AS2914) to Sucuri.
5. **Other direct foreign** - zameen (AWS), ilmkidunya (OVH), sadapay (Cloudflare Spectrum).

## Key takeaway

Nayatel is **not just a Transworld transit customer**. It is effectively **multi-homed**:
direct peering with the big content networks (Cloudflare, Microsoft, AWS) and
alternative international transit (SingTel, NTT) that bypass Transworld. It only
falls back to **Transworld** for the long tail of foreign hosts it does not peer with
(Hetzner, Oracle, Akamai, Incapsula, generic clouds) and for a few PK ISPs (NTC, etc.)
it does not peer with domestically. That is why everyday sites are ~3 ms yet ~40% of
paths still show Transworld - the 40% is essentially the foreign-hosted tail.

## Caveats

- Single snapshot (one traceroute per destination from one Nayatel probe in Islamabad).
- Transit attribution uses RDAP allocation (per-IP), which is the registered holder
  - strong but a /16 can contain customer sub-allocations.
- `HTISPL-PK` is a Pakistani network used to reach COMSATS/Multinet; its exact role
  (peering fabric vs small transit) is not pinned down here.
