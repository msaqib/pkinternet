# Findings 01 — Hosting & Routing of Top Pakistani Websites

**Dataset:** 91 unique websites, 393 traceroute measurements, 5 probes/ISPs
(Transworld ×2, PTCL, Nayatel, Z-Com), batches 2–11.
**Method:** ICMP Paris traceroute from each probe → each site; origin network via
Team Cymru ASN (+ RDAP fallback); actual location via ip-api (server IP for
unicast, handoff hop for anycast CDNs). See `experiments/01_website_destinations`.

> Charts for these findings are generated by
> `findings/01_hosting_and_routing.ipynb`.

---

## 1. Hosting census — only ~25% of top PK sites are hosted in Pakistan

| Where it's hosted | Sites | Share |
|---|---|---|
| **Real server in Pakistan** | 23 | 25% |
| **Real server abroad** | 26 | 29% |
| **Anycast CDN** (origin hidden) | 42 | 46% |

~75% of top Pakistani websites are **not** on a Pakistani server — they sit on a
foreign CDN (mostly Cloudflare: 38 of 42) or a foreign cloud.

## 2. Strong split by sector

| Category | PK-hosted | CDN | Foreign server |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **Government** (18) | **13** | 4 | 1 |
| News (16) | 1 | 11 | 4 |
| Banking (13) | 1 | 7 | 5 |
| Ecommerce (15) | 1 | 7 | 7 |
| Education (12) | 3 | 6 | 3 |
| Telecom (9) | 4 | 2 | 3 |
| Health (3) | 0 | 3 | 0 |

**Government is the only sector that mostly stays in-country** (NTC, Cybernet,
PERN, COMSATS, PITB, PITC, FBR, Multinet). The consumer internet — news, banking,
shopping — has largely left.

## 3. Notable foreign-hosting patterns

- **Banks hosted/fronted entirely abroad:** Bank Alfalah → Dubai (Microsoft),
  Easypaisa → Singapore (AWS), Faysal → Florida, HabibMetro → New York,
  SadaPay → US (Cloudflare Spectrum). A regulated, sensitive sector sitting
  offshore.
- **Pakistani telecom companies host their *own* sites abroad:** brain.net → US,
  **wateen.com → Jakarta**, **zong.com.pk → Singapore**. (PTCL, Nayatel,
  StormFiber, Nexlinx self-host in PK — so it's a choice, not a necessity.)
- **News on Hetzner, Finland:** 24News, Daily Pakistan, Nawa-i-Waqt.
- **Ecommerce scattered across AWS regions:** Daraz → Singapore, Khaadi → Muscat,
  OLX → Bahrain, Sargodha → Seattle, Gharbaar → Mumbai, Zameen → Dublin.
- Foreign footprint spans **10 countries** (US 7, SG 4, CA 3, FI 3, IE 2, AE 2,
  ID 2, IN/BH/OM 1).

## 4. CDN delivery — mostly a local edge, with a real hairpin tail

Where the probe *enters* the CDN, across all CDN measurements:

| Enters CDN at | Measurements |
|---|---:|
| Karachi / Islamabad (PK) | 94 |
| Singapore / Hong Kong | 12 |
| Turkey / US / Germany / UK | 9 |

Cloudflare-fronted sites usually reach a local PK edge, but ~18% of the time
traffic enters the CDN *abroad*. **Caveat:** entering the CDN locally ≠ HTTP
served locally — the true PoP can still be abroad (proven by `shaukatkhanum.org.pk`,
which entered Cloudflare at ~4 ms from Lahore but returned `colo=SIN`). This is the
network-edge view, not authoritative serving location.

## 5. Per-ISP routing quality — Nayatel is in a different league

Destination RTT by ISP:

| ISP | median | p90 | >100 ms (hairpin) | <20 ms (local) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Nayatel** | **4.4 ms** | 179 | 23% | **57%** |
| Transworld | 27 ms | 177 | 29% | 24% |
| Z-Com | 36 ms | 196 | 25% | 46% |
| TPCPL | 38 ms | 179 | 26% | 22% |
| PTCL | 44 ms | 461 | 38% | 25% |

Nayatel reaches **57% of destinations in under 20 ms** — strong local peering (a
Set-3-looking ISP). Every ISP hairpins **23–38%** of traffic abroad.
*(Caveats: PTCL's extreme tail reflects its Docker-probe anomaly and it was
offline for several batches, n=55; filtered probes can make non-responding
destinations look deceptively local.)*

## 6. Core finding — same destination, wildly different ISP

For the **same site**, destination RTT swings by hundreds of ms depending on the
ISP:

- **Cloudflare banking sites** (SadaPay, BankIslami, Meezan, Askari, JazzCash,
  NayaPay): **Nayatel 3–4 ms** vs **PTCL 250–935 ms** — a 100–200× gap to the
  *same* site.
- **Reversed for some PK-hosted gov sites** (pakistan.gov.pk, railways.gov.pk)
  and symbios.pk: **PTCL/Transworld reach them locally (~1 ms)** while
  Nayatel/Z-Com hairpin at **100–227 ms**.

**There is no universally "best" ISP.** Local reachability depends on whether
that specific ISP peers with the destination's network — exactly the bilateral
peering / PKIX-underuse story. Pairs that peer get single-digit ms; pairs that
don't bounce abroad — *for the same domestic content*. Uniform local exchange
(a fully-used IXP) would flatten these differences.

## 7. Transit hierarchy and hairpinning (robust, path-based)

These results are **RTT-independent** (derived from the AS/country path, not delay)
and are the firmest evidence in this experiment. The Shaw/Canada hop (AS6327, on the
TPCPL probe) is a known artifact and is **excluded**.

**Transit dependency** - share of each ISP's paths that traverse an LDI operator
(PTCL AS17557 or Transworld AS38193):

| ISP | via an LDI |
|---|---|
| Z-Com | 100% (91/91) |
| TPCPL | 100% (65/65) |
| Nayatel | 6% (6/91) |
| Transworld | 0% (is an LDI) |
| PTCL | 0% (is an LDI) |

Downstream ISPs route essentially **everything** through the two international-transit
providers; **Nayatel is the exception**, mostly on independent peering.

**Hairpinning is concentrated, not pervasive.** Of the 23 Pakistan-hosted sites, only
**five** are reached via a foreign hop, and only by the downstream ISPs - never by
PTCL or Transworld:

| PK-hosted site | hairpinned via | why |
|---|---|---|
| pakistan.gov.pk | Nayatel, TPCPL, Z-Com | Cybernet origin fronted by US Akamai Prolexic |
| moitt.gov.pk | Nayatel, TPCPL, Z-Com | same |
| railways.gov.pk | Nayatel, TPCPL, Z-Com | same |
| pitc.com.pk | Nayatel, TPCPL, Z-Com | routes via US Cogent (AS174) |
| goto.com.pk | Nayatel, TPCPL, Z-Com | similar foreign transit |

PTCL/Transworld have domestic routes to these; the downstream ISPs send them abroad.
That is the no-local-peering / PKIX-underuse signature, shown cleanly.

## Limitations (be vigilant when quoting RTT)

- **RTT is a single packet** (the first reply per hop), not the minimum of several,
  so it carries jitter. Best practice is min-of-N; we can't recompute it without
  re-fetching raw results. Prefer **medians** and **path-based metrics** (sections
  6-7) over individual RTT values.
- **Selection bias:** the set of destinations that *respond* differs by probe, so a
  per-ISP "average RTT" compares different destination sets. PTCL's high responded
  median is partly because the sites it reaches fastest (DDoS-scrubbed gov sites at
  ~1 ms) do not reply and are excluded. Nayatel's lead, however, holds.
- **Shaw/Canada artifact (AS6327, TPCPL probe only, all 65 measurements)** is
  physically in Pakistan; excluded from all path/hairpinning analysis.
- CDN locations are network-edge inferences, not authoritative `colo`.
- Apex-only: CDN-fronted orgs may run in-country servers on subdomains we don't
  measure (e.g. shaukatkhanum's `hmis`/`mhmis` on PTCL).
- PTCL and TPCPL probes have partial coverage (intermittently offline; smaller n).

## Bottom line

Pakistani **government keeps its data in-country, but the consumer internet
(news, banking, shopping) is ~75% offshore**, and domestic routing quality is
**brutally ISP-dependent** — direct measurement evidence that PKIX-style local
exchange is not happening uniformly.
