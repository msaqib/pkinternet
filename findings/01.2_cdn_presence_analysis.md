# Findings 1.2 - CDN / Cache Presence in Pakistan

**Author:** Rayan Atif
**Dataset:** 20 major content services pinged from each probe with
`resolve_on_probe=True` (run_20260606_cdn1). 3 probes responded (Nayatel,
Transworld, Z-Com); PTCL and TPCPL were offline. See `experiments/01.2_cdn_presence/`.

## Objective

Test whether big content providers (Netflix, Google/YouTube, Meta, Akamai,
Cloudflare, Microsoft, Apple, ...) serve from a cache **inside Pakistan**, prompted
by Dr Saqib's note that Netflix/Akamai/Meta/Google likely have embedded caches in
Pakistani ISPs. Verdict per (service, probe) from the RTT: **PK-local (<15 ms) /
regional (<50 ms) / abroad (>=50 ms)**.

## Headline

From our three ISPs, **almost no major global content is served from inside
Pakistan.** Only **Cloudflare and X (Twitter)** were PK-local, and **only via
Nayatel** (3-4 ms). Everything else is **regional** (~30-50 ms, likely Gulf or
Singapore edges) or **abroad** (>90 ms). Verdict tally across 60 service-probe
pairs: **2 PK-local, 29 regional, 20 abroad, 9 no-reply** (ICMP-filtered).

## Observations

1. **Nayatel is again the standout** - the only ISP with a truly local (single-digit
   ms) reach, and only to Cloudflare and X. Consistent with Experiment 01.
2. **Meta is uniformly regional (~30-38 ms)** across all three ISPs - a nearby edge
   (Gulf/Singapore), not inside Pakistan, but not far.
3. **Transworld routes Google very far (~323-325 ms abroad)** while Nayatel and
   Z-Com get it regional (~30-40 ms) - a large per-ISP routing difference for the
   same service.
4. **Netflix, Apple, Wikipedia, GitHub are abroad (>90 ms)** from all three ISPs.
5. **ICMP-filtered (no reply):** amazon.com, microsoft.com, netflix.com - ping
   cannot measure these.

## Important caveats (this likely undercounts in-PK caches)

- **Wrong ISPs for the question.** The ISPs Dr Saqib named as cache hosts (PTCL,
  Optix, StormFiber, Fiberlink, Telenor) are **not among our probes**; PTCL/TPCPL
  were offline. We only see Nayatel, Transworld, Z-Com. Embedded caches on other
  ISPs are invisible to us. **This is the dominant limitation** and is exactly what
  Experiment 02's probe deployment is meant to fix.
- **We test the website/control endpoint, not the video cache.** Netflix Open
  Connect serves *video* (`*.nflxvideo.net`), and Google video caches serve
  `*.googlevideo.com` content - the embedded caches are reached through the app's
  streaming flow, which `fast.com` / `netflix.com` / a plain `youtube.com` ping does
  not exercise. So "Netflix abroad" here is **inconclusive for OCA presence**, not a
  refutation of it.
- **Ping-based** detection misses ICMP-filtered services entirely.
- **RTT thresholds are heuristic;** "regional" (~30-50 ms) most likely means a Gulf
  or Singapore edge, not Pakistan, but is not pinned to a city here.

## Bottom line

For the three ISPs we can currently see, the big content providers are **mostly
reached regionally or abroad, not from inside Pakistan** - with Cloudflare/X via
Nayatel the only local exceptions. This does **not** disprove Dr Saqib's expected
Netflix/Google/Meta caches: we lack probes on the ISPs said to host them, and our
ping method does not hit the actual video-cache endpoints. The clean next steps are
(1) deploy probes on the cache-hosting ISPs (Exp 02), and (2) test the real cache
hostnames (`*.nflxvideo.net`, `*.googlevideo.com`) rather than the front-page.
