# Findings 1.1 - Per-ISP DNS Resolution

**Author:** Rayan Atif
**Dataset:** 103 sites resolved from each probe's own ISP resolver (RIPE Atlas DNS,
`use_probe_resolver`), run_20260606_dns. 3 probes responded (Nayatel, Transworld,
Z-Com); PTCL and TPCPL were offline. See `experiments/01.1_dns_resolution/`.

## Why this experiment exists

Experiment 01 resolved each hostname **once, centrally**, and sent that single IP
to all probes. That is blind to **GeoDNS**: services that hand different IPs to
different ISPs. This experiment resolves per-ISP and measures how often, and how
much, that matters.

## Headline

**Only 8 of 103 sites (~8%) returned different IPs across ISPs.** The other ~92%
resolved to the **same IP from every responding ISP** - so Experiment 01's central
lookup was representative for the large majority, and its hosting census stands.

## The 8 GeoDNS sites, and what actually differs

| Site | Category | Provider | What differs across ISPs |
|---|---|---|---|
| bisp.gov.pk | government | Cloudflare | different Cloudflare **edge IP ranges** (Z-Com on 188.114.x, others on 104/172.67.x) |
| bykea.com | food | Cloudflare | same, different Cloudflare edge ranges |
| ishopping.pk | ecommerce | Cloudflare | same |
| khaadi.com | ecommerce | Amazon CloudFront | different CloudFront edge IPs |
| olx.com.pk | ecommerce | Amazon CloudFront | different CloudFront edge IPs |
| nadra.gov.pk | government | Akamai | different Akamai edge IPs per ISP |
| lums.edu.pk | education | PERN/Transworld | **Transworld resolver returns a private `10.99.0.24`** (internal/split-DNS view); Nayatel/Z-Com get the public PK IPs |
| nayatel.com | telecom | Nayatel | **Z-Com gets a DigitalOcean (foreign) IP** alongside the PK one; others get PK IPs |

## Interpretation (low impact, with two interesting exceptions)

- **6 of the 8** are just **different edge IPs of the same global CDN**
  (Cloudflare, Amazon CloudFront, Akamai). The provider - and the hosting-country
  verdict - is unchanged; only the specific edge differs per ISP. So even for these,
  Experiment 01's classification is essentially correct.
- **Two are genuinely interesting:**
  - **lums.edu.pk** - the Transworld probe's resolver returns an **RFC1918 private
    IP** (`10.99.0.24`), i.e. an internal/split-DNS view, while other ISPs get the
    public PERN/Transworld IPs. A site that looks different from inside one network.
  - **nayatel.com** - **Z-Com** is handed a **DigitalOcean (foreign) IP**, whereas
    Nayatel and Transworld resolve it to Pakistani Nayatel IPs. One ISP would have
    been measured reaching it abroad.

## Bottom line

The central-resolution shortcut in Experiment 01 is a **real but low-impact**
limitation: per-ISP DNS differences exist for ~8% of sites, and for most of those
the difference is a different edge of the same CDN (no change to hosting country).
The two cases worth following up are `lums.edu.pk` (split-DNS) and `nayatel.com`
(one ISP sees a foreign IP). Experiment 01's hosting census is unaffected.

## Limitations

- **3 of 5 probes** responded (PTCL, TPCPL offline). Re-run when they are up for
  full coverage; the ISPs reportedly hosting embedded caches (Optix, StormFiber,
  Telenor, ...) are not among our probes at all.
- **Single snapshot.** DNS answers can also change over time; re-run on a schedule
  to capture temporal variation.
- A "different IP" comparison uses exact IP sets, so CDN IP rotation within one
  query can register as a difference even when the serving edge is the same.
